What is Binary Search?

Binary Search works on a sorted array and repeatedly divides the search space in half.

👉 Instead of checking every element (like linear search), it eliminates half each time.

How Binary Search Works:_____
Initialize: Set two pointers, low at the first index and high at the last index of the array.

Find Midpoint: Calculate the middle index: mid = low + (high - low) / 2.

Compare:

If the middle element equals the target, return its index.

If the target is smaller than the middle element, discard the right half by setting high = mid - 1.

If the target is larger than the middle element, discard the left half by setting low = mid + 1.

Repeat: Continue steps 2 and 3 until the target is found or low exceeds high (meaning the target is not present).
